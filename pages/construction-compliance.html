Ensure Construction Compliance in South Africa

Construction sites in South Africa must comply with the Occupational Health and Safety Act, 1993 (Act No. 85 of 1993). This tool helps clients and contractors meet their legal obligations under Section 8 (General duties of employers to their employees) and Section 9 (General duties of employers and self-employed persons to persons other than their employees). Use the checklists and forms below to ensure your site is safe and compliant.

Step 2: Select Your Role

Are you a client (project owner) or a contractor? Select your role to proceed with the compliance process.

Client Responsibilities

As a client, you play a critical role in ensuring construction safety. Section 8(2)(e) of the OHS Act requires you to ensure that contractors are competent and that the site is safe for workers.

Client Compliance Checklist

  • Select a competent contractor with the necessary skills and resources.
  • Ensure a capable management structure is in place to oversee the project.
  • Monitor the progress of the project to ensure safety standards are met.
  • Provide welfare facilities (e.g., sanitation, rest areas) for workers.

Contractor Responsibilities

Contractors must comply with the OHS Act and its regulations, including the Construction Regulations, 2014. Regulation 5 requires contractors to ensure safety measures are in place to protect workers and the public.

Contractor Compliance Checklist

  • Implement safety regulations (e.g., PPE, fall protection).
  • Ensure equipment complies with safety standards.
  • Conduct regular safety inspections and risk assessments.
  • Train workers on safety procedures and emergency protocols.

Create a Health and Safety Plan

Key Legislation for Construction Compliance

The Construction Regulations, 2014 (under the OHS Act) outline specific duties for construction sites. Regulation 5(1) states: “A contractor must ensure that all work is carried out under his supervision and that all risks are assessed and mitigated.”
