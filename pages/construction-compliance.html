Legal Requirements for Construction Compliance in South Africa

The Occupational Health and Safety Act, 1993 (Act No. 85 of 1993), and the Construction Regulations, 2014, establish a legal framework to ensure safety on construction sites in South Africa. Both clients (project owners) and contractors have specific duties to protect workers, visitors, and the public.

Client Responsibilities

Clients are obligated under Section 8(2)(e) of the OHS Act to ensure that contractors are competent and that the site is safe. The Construction Regulations further specify:

  • CR 5(1)(a): Prepare a health and safety specification outlining safety requirements for the project.
  • CR 5(1)(b): Submit a Notification of Construction Work (Annexure 2) to the Department of Labour before work begins.
  • CR 5(1)(k): Appoint a competent principal contractor to manage safety on-site.
  • CR 5(1)(f): Ensure the contractor complies with the OHS Act and regulations throughout the project.
