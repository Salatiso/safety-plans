Legal Requirements for Construction Compliance in South Africa

Under the Occupational Health and Safety Act, 1993 (Act No. 85 of 1993), both clients and contractors have specific legal obligations to ensure a safe construction site. The Construction Regulations, 2014 further outline these responsibilities to protect workers and the public.

Client Responsibilities

Clients (project owners) are required to comply with Section 8(2)(e) of the OHS Act, which mandates ensuring that contractors are competent and that the site is safe for workers. Additionally, Regulation 5(1)(a) of the Construction Regulations requires clients to:

  • Prepare a health and safety specification for the project.
  • Appoint a competent principal contractor (Regulation 5(1)(k)).
  • Ensure the contractor complies with the OHS Act and regulations.

Contractor Responsibilities

Contractors must adhere to Section 9 of the OHS Act, ensuring their activities do not endanger others. Regulation 5(1) of the Construction Regulations further requires contractors to:

  • Develop a health and safety plan based on the client’s specification (Regulation 5(1)(b)).
  • Conduct risk assessments and implement safety measures (Regulation 5(1)(q)).
  • Ensure all work is supervised and risks are mitigated (Regulation 5(1)).

Failure to comply with these regulations can result in penalties, project delays, or legal action. Use the tools below to ensure compliance with South African health and safety legislation.

How This Tool Helps You

Safety First OHS Tools simplifies construction compliance for both clients and contractors. This tool can help you generate the following key documents required by the Occupational Health and Safety Act, 1993, and the Construction Regulations, 2014:

  • Health and Safety Specification: A document outlining safety requirements for the project (for clients).
  • Baseline Risk Assessment: An assessment to identify and mitigate risks on the site (for contractors).
  • Health and Safety Plan: A detailed plan to ensure safety compliance during the project (for contractors).
  • Other Required Documentation: Additional documents such as training records, equipment safety checklists, and permit applications (for both clients and contractors).

Key Legislation for Construction Compliance

The Construction Regulations, 2014 (under the OHS Act) outline specific duties for construction sites. Regulation 5(1) states: “A contractor must ensure that all work is carried out under his supervision and that all risks are assessed and mitigated.”
